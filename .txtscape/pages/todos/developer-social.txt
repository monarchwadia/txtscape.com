# Developer Social Network via txtscape

Status: not started

Users read each other's public updates, follow projects they care about, and get LLM-generated digests of activity from people they follow. Decentralized by nature — content lives on txtscape.com as plain .txt files, agents do the reading and summarizing.

## Vision
A social layer for developers, powered entirely by agents reading plain text. No algorithmic feed, no engagement metrics, no ads. Agents surf txtscape.com/~username/ pages, find interesting updates, and bring digests back to the developer. The local GUI can surface this too.

## How it works
- Users publish project updates to txtscape.com (see: public-updates todo)
- Other users' agents discover and read these via surf()
- Agents summarize what's interesting and surface it locally
- The local GUI can show a feed of followed projects
- Follow lists could be stored locally in .txtscape (e.g. follows.txt)

## Decentralization
- Content is plain .txt on HTTPS — anyone can mirror, cache, archive
- No central algorithm decides what you see — your agent does
- Follow lists are local, not stored on a server
- No engagement tracking, no metrics, no advertising
- The protocol is simple enough that alternative servers can exist
