Expanding the World

One room is not much of an adventure. Let's build a world.

Add more rooms

Tell your agent
"Create these txtscape pages:

rooms/forest.txt:
The Dark Forest is thick with ancient oaks. Moonlight barely pierces the canopy. Strange sounds echo between the trees. A narrow path winds deeper into the darkness.
Exits: south leads to the Rusty Flagon. East leads to the Crystal Cave.
Items: a torn map lies half-buried in the leaves.

rooms/cave.txt:
The Crystal Cave glitters with phosphorescent minerals. Stalactites drip into still pools of water. Deep in the cave, a faint blue glow pulses rhythmically.
Exits: west leads to the Dark Forest.
Items: a glowing gem sits on a natural stone pedestal."

The agent calls put_page twice, creating two new pages in the rooms/ folder. Your game world now has three connected locations: 

Tavern --north--> Forest --east--> Cave

Add a character

Tell your agent
"Create a txtscape page at characters/bartender.txt:

Grimjaw is the bartender at the Rusty Flagon. He's seen everything and trusts no one. He has a gravelly voice and a scar across his left eye.

Dialogue: 'Careful in those woods, stranger. People go in. Not all of 'em come back.'

Knows about: the Crystal Cave, the glowing gem, and an old legend about the Eldorian kings."

Notice the folder structure that's forming:

.txtscape/pages/
├── rooms/
│   ├── tavern.txt
│   ├── forest.txt
│   └── cave.txt
└── characters/
    └── bartender.txt

Rooms in one folder, characters in another. The organization is natural — you chose folder names that match your domain. txtscape doesn't enforce a structure; you shape it to fit your project.

Regenerate the game

Now ask the agent to rebuild the game using all the knowledge:

Tell your agent
"Use txtscape's snapshot tool to read all pages at once. Then update index.html with all three rooms connected by their exits, the bartender NPC with a talk button that shows his dialogue, and an inventory system so the player can pick up and carry items."

The agent calls snapshot — a single tool call that loads every page. It now has full context: three rooms with their connections, a character with dialogue, and items scattered across locations.

Open index.html. You should be able to:

  • Walk from the tavern to the forest to the cave and back
  • Pick up items — the rusty sword, torn map, glowing gem
  • Talk to Grimjaw and hear his warning

All of it came from your txtscape pages. The agent read plain text and produced a working game.

Snapshot vs. individual reads: snapshot loads all pages in one call — efficient when the agent needs full context. get_page reads one page at a time — better for targeted lookups. The agent will pick the right tool based on what you ask.
